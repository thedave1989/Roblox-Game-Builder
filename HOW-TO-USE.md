# How to Use Your Game Builder 🎮

Hi! This is your game-making helper. You talk to it, and together you build
real Roblox games. No coding needed — that's its job.

## Starting up (every time)

1. Open **Roblox Studio** and open your game.
2. Open **Claude Code** (Dave showed you how — it's the chat window).
3. Say hi, or just type a command. It remembers where you left off.

## The magic words

| Type this | And this happens |
| --- | --- |
| **/newgame** | Tell it your game idea. It asks a few questions and makes a plan. |
| **/build** | It builds the next piece of your game. |
| **/test** | It shows you how to try the new piece. You press Play! |
| **/fix** | Something weird? Broken? Type this and describe it. |
| **/undo** | Go back to how things were before. Nothing is ever lost. |
| **/publish** | Put your game on Roblox so people can play it! |
| **/help** | Shows you this list, and what to do next. |

## The rhythm 🥁

**/build** … then **/test** … then **/build** … then **/test** …

One piece at a time. Every piece is something you can SEE in your game right
away. Before you know it — whole game.

## The three golden rules

1. **You can't break it.** Your work is saved automatically, always. Worst
   case, /undo takes you back.
2. **Always /test after /build.** Seeing it work is the fun part anyway.
3. **Stuck? Say so.** Type /fix and describe it YOUR way ("the door ate me").
   That's a perfectly good bug report.

## When to ask Dave

If the helper says **"ask Dave to look at this"** — that's not you failing,
that's the helper being honest. Send Dave the little hint it gives you.

Also ask Dave if Claude says it needs a break (you used up the day's chatting)
and you're not sure when to come back.

Have fun. Make something weird. 🚀
