# Audio licensing reality

- The **Creator Store** (Roblox's built-in asset marketplace, what used to
  be called the Toolbox audio library) has a large catalogue of audio that
  is free and pre-cleared to use in any Roblox experience. This is the
  default, safest source for this framework's games — insert straight from
  there, no extra steps.
- Anything **not** from the Creator Store — a song downloaded off YouTube, a
  sound effect pulled from some other website — has to be **uploaded to
  Roblox as an Audio asset** before Studio can use it at all, and Roblox
  moderates every upload (for both copyright and content-appropriateness)
  before it's usable. Uploading is done through Dave's own Roblox account —
  it's a real account/ownership action, not something the builder agent
  does or works around.
- In practice, most third-party audio a kid finds online (a specific
  copyrighted song, especially) either won't pass moderation or risks being
  taken down later. So the working policy here is: **use Creator Store audio
  by default; a specific song/sound found elsewhere is a "let's ask Dave"
  moment**, not something to fetch or paste in.

_verified: 2026-07-17 — Creator Store terms, upload/moderation flow, and
exact free-use scope change over time; confirm current policy at
create.roblox.com/docs before relying on specifics beyond "ask Dave for
anything not already in the Creator Store"._
