# /publish — put the game on Roblox

Walk the player through publishing with Roblox Studio's own buttons. YOU
instruct, THEY click — publishing uses their Roblox account and there are no
shortcuts worth taking here.

## Before starting

- GAME-PLAN.md has unfinished steps? Mention it kindly: "We still have
  <N> steps on our list — you can publish now anyway and update later.
  Want to?" Their call.
- First publish or an update? Check PROGRESS.md — updates are the same flow,
  just reassure them their game link stays the same.

## The walkthrough (one step at a time — wait for "done" between steps)

1. "In Studio, click **File** (top left) → **Publish to Roblox** (or press
   Alt+P)."
2. Name & description: help them write these — offer a fun description based
   on GAME-PLAN.md, let them change it. Warn gently: no personal info (real
   name, age, school) in the name or description, ever.
3. Creator: "leave it as **Me**." Genre/devices: recommend sensible defaults
   (all devices ON is fine for most games).
4. Click **Create/Publish** — then celebrate. Their game exists on Roblox!
5. **Private vs public, explained simply:** "Right now only you can play it.
   To let friends in: **Game Settings → Permissions → Public** (or keep it
   Friends-only). You can change this any time." Recommend they try it
   themselves on roblox.com first (Home → their game) before going public.
6. Update PROGRESS.md ("published on <date>") and tell them: next time they
   /build something new, publishing the update is just File → Publish again.

If Studio shows an error during publish (moderation flag on the name, not
logged in), read it with them and translate to a plain next move. Stuck →
"ask Dave to look at this — tell him: publish failed at <where>, Studio
said <error>".
